01

The problem

Public trash bins get collected on fixed routes, not by need — so crews empty bins that aren't full and miss ones that are, wasting labor and leaving public spaces overflowing. The goal: collect and surface real-time fill data to sanitation workers, notifying trash services via an app when a bin actually needs emptying, and scale that across public areas with Wi-Fi access to keep spaces clean while using sanitation labor efficiently.

02

How it works

Sense

Laser fill sensors

Laser sensors mounted in each bin read fill level continuously, paired with a Wi-Fi-enabled logic board for connectivity.

Connect

Wi-Fi logic board

Bins in public Wi-Fi zones stream status to the platform — no dedicated network buildout required.

Act

Crew mobile app

Sanitation workers get app notifications when a bin crosses its threshold, turning fixed routes into need-based collection.

03

Plan & approach

Structure

Work Breakdown Structure

Every deliverable decomposed into work packages — sensor R&D, app development, field testing and deployment — each carrying a 15% contingency for traceability.

Time

Schedule & Gantt

Activities sequenced into a Gantt with dependencies across seven packages (A–G), driving a 136-day end-to-end timeline.

Money

Cost model

  • Engineering — sensor R&D, integration & testing, costed on scheduled labor hours.
  • Manufacturing — sensors and Wi-Fi logic boards.
  • Labor rates from ZipRecruiter engineering averages; +15% contingency on total.

05

Schedule recovery & risk

Recover

Schedule-recovery plan

A defined recovery plan to protect the 99-day path against slippage — fast-tracking parallelizable work and crashing critical activities with added resource where cost-justified.

Buffer

Risk contingency

A 15% contingency carried on every WBS element and on total cost, mapped back to specific work packages so budget and schedule buffers stay traceable.

06

Resources & stakeholders

Resource management

Mapped to the WBS

  • People — engineering team, app developers, support staff.
  • Vendors — laser-sensor and Wi-Fi-module suppliers.
  • Materials — laser sensors, logic boards, integration components.
  • Every resource mapped to its work package for clear ownership.
Stakeholder engagement

Aligned & informed

  • Primary client — sanitation department officials.
  • End-users — sanitation workers; suppliers for components.
  • Bi-weekly reviews and feedback sessions for alignment.
  • Webex & Slack plus informal channels to clear roadblocks fast and avoid siloed data.
07

Outcome

The program delivers a fully costed, schedule-ready plan to make trash collection and sanitation-labor usage need-based instead of route-based — keeping public areas cleaner while cutting wasted trips. Headline numbers: $245,686 all-in (with contingency) across a 136-day timeline anchored by a 99-day critical path, ready to pilot in high-traffic Wi-Fi zones before scaling.
