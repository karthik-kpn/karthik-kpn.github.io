01

The problem

UMass transit riders wait through brutal New England winters on cold metal benches that go almost unused — under 10% utilization. Riders stand in the cold instead of sitting on freezing, ice-covered, ergonomically poor seating, hurting comfort, accessibility, and the campus experience at high-traffic transit hubs.

02

Product vision & solution

WarmStop reimagines the transit bench as a comfortable, all-weather, accessible-by-design seat that people actually want to use in winter.

Comfort

Heated & ergonomic

Heated HDPE surface with lumbar support and cushioning — warm, supportive seating built for sub-freezing waits.

Durability

Ice-shedding & ADA

Hydrophobic surface sheds ice and water; ADA compliance designed in from the start, not bolted on.

Future-ready

Solar-ready & smart

Solar-ready energy with a path to LED safety lighting and sensor data — a campus brand differentiator.

04

Market & business case

WarmStop targets UMass Amherst's 30,000+ daily commuters with a clear expansion path across PVTA transit and the Five College Consortium. The case was pressure-tested with market opportunity & sizing, a 5C analysis, and PESTLE & SWOT — framing demand, competitive context, and the macro factors (climate, accessibility regulation, campus reputation) that make heated, ADA-ready seating a defensible bet.

05

Success metrics & KPIs

Primary KPI

Utilization > 60%

Lift bench utilization from under 10% today to a 60%+ winter target — the headline success metric and the gate to Phase 2.

Operational

Less standing congestion

Reduce crowding and standing at transit hubs as seating becomes usable through the cold months.

Evidence

Winter feedback loop

Capture rider feedback (QR codes) and utilization data each winter to feed iteration and the scale-up decision.
